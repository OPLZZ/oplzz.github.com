Why to go at first day

    • 09:00 - 12:00
    • Advertising through structured data on the Web
    • Jindřich Mynarz, Josef Petrák
    • workshop I. - 30 tables in czech (česky)
    • The hands­on workshop introduces ways how to describe content of web sites as structured data using mark­up embedded in HTML. Exposing structured semantic data on the Web can serve as digital advertising through third party applications, such as search engines or social media. The techniques of advertising through structured data on the Web will be presented on example job postings described in terms of the Schema.org vocabulary.
    • 12:00 - 14:00
    • coffe break/lunch time
    • 14:00 - 17:00 downstairs
    • Querying across internal and external data sources using Linked Data
    • Richard Cyganiak, Anna Dabrowska
    • workshop II. - 30 tables
    • With W3C's Linked Data standards, organisations can get a unified view over their various data sources, and combine them with a growing body of open data that is published on the Web. We show how these standards can be used to mash up data from spreadsheets and databases in web­based tools.
    • 14:00 - 17:00 upstairs
    • Business data analytics for personalisation using the social and interest graph
    • Benjamin Heitmann, Maciej Dabrowski
    • workshop III. - 50 tables
    • Current Business Intelligence solutions tend to exploit numerous domains and sources of data, such as eCommerce platform logs (infrastructure), user trails form social networks (marketing), or supply chain management (logistics). However, contemporary business analytics tools do not exploit the full potential of available data sources related to implicit connections between datasets. Instead, graph­based algorithms can generate valuable insights and add value by exploiting the structure/connections between different datasets. Graph­based analytics can provide both high­level KPIs (e.g. higher conversion) as well as personal insights (e.g. higher individual sales with product recommendations).

      This tutorial will introduce recent advances in graph­based business analytics approaches that exploit large social and interest graphs. We will show how to use and link external (Web) datasets with internal data sources to increase BI capabilities. You will learn (1) how to use existing tools to access multiple graph data sources, (2) build a simple recommender system to provide personalisation capabilities.

Brand reputation tracking using multiple social networking platforms

Lukasz Porwol
The proliferation of Social Media analytic tools in recent years opens new opportunities for business to re­connect with the customer. Many of the current solutions explore existing APIs and export vast amounts of data without extra effort put on relevant data consolidation from multiple sources. In this talk we elaborate on the use of Semantic Web technologies to integrate data from well­established social networking platforms in Social Media Linked Data Space (SMLDS). We discuss how to effectively explore the created knowledge graph. We show the benefits of SMLDS based on the recent implementation for brand reputation tracking project.

Towards Analytics on Top of Big RDF Data

Fadi Maali
Data is playing a crucial role in societies, governments and enterprises. For instance, data science is increasingly utilised in supporting data­driven decisions and in delivering data products. Furthermore, scientific fields such as bioinformatics, astronomy and oceanography are going through a shift from “querying the world” to “querying the data” in what commonly referred to as e­science. RDF, being a flexible model for data on the Web, has great role to play in enabling this revolution. In this talk, I will present recent efforts in utilising RDF data in analytics tasks and discuss the relevant challenges and opportunities.

Business models for linked data

Jan Kučera
Linked data represents technologies, standards and methods that allow organization and interlinking of data from different sources across various domains. Although the application of linked data principles promises significant benefits, its adoption by the enterprises is sometimes seen as rather slow. In this talk we will discuss the benefits associated with linked data and we will present a set of business models that might be adopted by the linked data providers and some other types of actors on the linked data marketplace.
